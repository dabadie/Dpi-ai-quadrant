Methodology

Scoring Framework

Each dimension is scored from 0 to 5. Scores are normalized and combined using weighted sums to produce DPI and AI maturity scores on a 0–100 scale.

Formula: weighted score = Σ((dimension score / 5) × dimension weight).

Quadrant Logic

  • Low DPI / Low AI: DPI < DPI threshold and AI < AI threshold
  • High DPI / Low AI: DPI ≥ DPI threshold and AI < AI threshold
  • Low DPI / High AI: DPI < DPI threshold and AI ≥ AI threshold
  • High DPI / High AI: DPI ≥ DPI threshold and AI ≥ AI threshold

The DPI Equator is a horizontal benchmark line (default 50) to indicate whether DPI foundations are sufficiently mature for scaled transformation.

The Golden Zone identifies countries with both strong DPI and AI maturity (default DPI ≥ 70 and AI ≥ 70).

DaaS Uplift Pathway

Data-as-a-Service (DaaS) can move countries upward on DPI maturity by improving secure identity-linked data exchange, consent architecture, and interoperability rails. This enables trusted, reusable workflows that AI systems can safely build on.

Country context variables (UN EGDI, mobile access, poverty, and inequality) should be interpreted with capacity and public-finance constraints to prioritize feasible implementation pathways.

AI Delivery Evaluation

AI maturity is assessed across public-sector enablers and delivery mechanics, including AI building blocks, DPI workflow integration, public-service agents, and model strategy choices (LLM vs SLM).

Capacity and financing dimensions are explicitly weighted in the DPI and AI models to better reflect delivery realism in low-resource settings.
